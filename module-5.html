Module 05 of 06 — Mum 180

I Remember
You

The mirror exercise. Michelle had everything. And she'd been gone for 19 years.

Time: 30–45 min
Sections: 4
Section 1
Michelle's Story

I had a woman in my course. My course was three weeks long. A beautiful French woman living now in Australia for the last 25 years. Let's call her Michelle. And from the beginning of my courses — my Life Circuitry course, which is the bigger personal development course which covers everything — she served as an example of this trap that mothers fall into.

It serves as a great example because it's not the typical single mum doing it hard on her own with all the additional challenges. Michelle was happily married for the last 20 years. Her and her husband were both successful in their careers, had a very comfortable lifestyle. Had a son who was 19 years old, who six months prior had moved out of home to start university. Apparently he's a really nice kid, doing very well academically. Looks like he's got a bright future.

Section 2
The Mirror Exercise

From the beginning of my courses, I encourage people — go to the mirror and have a conversation with yourself. You must set your standards with yourself early. You must recognise yourself as a human being and have that conversation with yourself. Not just how's my hair, brush my teeth. Really see yourself and acknowledge yourself as a person and someone that you care about, and set those standards about how you want to be seen and treated in the world.

There's a person waiting for you in your bathroom in a big silver box just above your sink. They love you and they care about you and they can't wait to see you. And we never talk to them.

If you can't handle the tension of having a conversation with yourself about the standards you're going to set for yourself, you can forget about getting the world to accept your standards.

Every day I'd ask: did anyone go to the mirror?

Section 3
I Remember You

On the last day of the course, Michelle comes in and she says: Alex, this morning I did what you've been talking about.

I said, oh, what's that? And she said, I went to the mirror to have a chat. And I said, wonderful, how did it go? And she said, I was very surprised, something unexpected happened.

She said, I sort of felt a bit silly. And I went over and I looked up and I saw myself. And before I knew what I was going to say, these words came out of my mouth.

"I pointed at the mirror and I said: I remember you."

And I said, wow. And I said, I bet I know how long you've been gone for. And at the same time, we both said: 19 years.

And I said, remind me, Michelle, how old's your son? And she said, yes, 19. And I said, what a coincidence.

And I said, so where have you been for the last 19 years? And she said: I don't know. When I became a mum, that's who I became.

Reflection
What comes up for you reading that story? Is there a version of yourself you've lost sight of?
Section 4
Your Turn

Tonight or tomorrow morning — go to the mirror. Not quickly. Not while doing something else. Just go. Look. See the person there.

Notice what comes up. You might feel silly. You might feel nothing. You might feel something unexpected. Whatever happens — write it down.

Reflection
Describe your experience with the mirror exercise. What did you notice? What came up?
Reflection
If you had to say — when did you last feel fully like yourself? What were you doing? What was different about that time?
