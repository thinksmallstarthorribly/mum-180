Module 03 of 06 — Mum 180

The Oxygen
Mask

Permission to put yourself first. Why it is the most responsible thing you can do as a parent.

Time: 25–35 min
Sections: 3
Section 1
The Analogy

When a plane goes down in the ocean, you don't put the oxygen mask on the child first, you put it on yourself. If you put it on the child first, the child stays alive long enough to watch you drown in front of them. And then they bob around for another 30 seconds before they die themselves.

You must serve your own life first before you can be of any service to anybody else or the commitments you've made.
Section 2
Full Permission

This module is giving you something formal. Permission. Not permission to be irresponsible. Not permission to abandon anyone.

Permission to exist as a full human being. Permission to have needs. Permission to take care of yourself — not as a reward you earn after everything else is done, but as a baseline requirement.

They are watching you. Right now. Learning from what you do, not from what you tell them. Whether you have any standards for your own care. Whether you treat yourself as someone who matters.

Section 3
Reflection
Reflection
Name one thing — small or large — that you have been denying yourself. What is it, and why have you been denying it?
Reflection
Do you genuinely believe that taking care of yourself is part of your commitment as a mother? What comes up when you try to accept that?
Reflection
What would you need to let go of to start putting your mask on first?
