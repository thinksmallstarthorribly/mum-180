Section 1 of 5
The Loop That Keeps You Stuck

There is a specific, predictable loop that operates underneath most patterns of stagnation. It has two phases. It runs on a fuel that looks like determination but functions as avoidance. And the reason most people can't escape it is that they're trying to solve the wrong problem.

"The reason you stopped is no longer the problem keeping you here."
Mum 180 · The Stuck Framework

The original problem — whatever stopped you the first time — has been replaced. What keeps you stuck now is the collapse of self-trust that happened when you stopped. You're not trying to solve the original problem. You're trying to function without trusting yourself. And that is a completely different problem with a completely different solution.

Section 2 of 5
The Threshold

Between where you are and where you want to be, there is a line. Most people can sense it — a point at which things would be fundamentally different. A point where the accumulated weight of the loop would be gone, where different behaviours would be possible, where the version of yourself you know exists would actually be running the show.

This is called The Threshold. And here is the critical truth about it: most people never cross it not because they can't, but because they don't know it's there. They keep stopping before it, interpreting the discomfort of approaching it as evidence they're going the wrong direction.

Section 3 of 5
Confidence vs. Self-Trust

This is the core distinction in the entire Mum 180 framework. Most personal development work focuses on building confidence. There's a fundamental problem with this: confidence is conditional.

Confidence is the positive emotional state that comes from favourable conditions — being in your area of expertise, having a recent win, feeling prepared, being in a good environment. It's real. It's useful. And it disappears the moment conditions change.

Self-trust is different. Self-trust is the belief, built from accumulated evidence of following through, that you will do what you say you will do. It doesn't require favourable conditions. It's portable. It works when confidence fails.

"Mum 180 doesn't build confidence. It rebuilds self-trust from evidence of what you've already proven you can do."

Section 4 of 5
The Evidence Builder

Self-trust is rebuilt from evidence. Not from motivation, affirmation, or renewed determination. From actual instances of following through, accumulated over time. The evidence doesn't have to be impressive. It has to be real.

The starting point is always the same: reduce the size of the action until the probability of following through is almost certain. A small action you actually take is worth infinitely more than a large action you don't. Each follow-through is a deposit in the self-trust account. Enough deposits and the balance changes the way you operate.

Build · Evidence Inventory
List every piece of evidence you have that you are capable of following through. Include things from years ago. Include things that feel small. Every instance counts.
Build · Your Minimum Viable Action
What is the smallest action you could take today that would constitute a deposit in your self-trust account? So small it's almost embarrassing. Define it precisely.
Section 5 of 5
Crossing the Threshold

The threshold is not crossed in one move. It's crossed through accumulated evidence of following through, built up until the self-trust account has enough in it to run your capability without needing conditions to be right first.

The process is: identify the minimum viable action → take it → record that you took it → repeat. The recording matters. Evidence doesn't build self-trust unless it's retrieved. Most people take the action and immediately discount it. The discipline of acknowledging the follow-through is part of the process.

Commit · Your Threshold Plan
Write your specific plan for the next 21 days. What is the one action you will take daily? How will you record it? What will you do on day 1, and what will "crossed" look like?
