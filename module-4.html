Section 01

Your Two States

You are not one consistent person. You operate in two states — a High-Capacity Self and a Low-Capacity Self. The problem isn't the low-capacity state. The problem is not knowing which state you're in, and therefore not knowing which decisions to make, which actions to take, or which version of yourself to trust.

The Foundation Circuit gap: The distance between who you know you can be and who you actually show up as on any given day. This module is about mapping that gap precisely — so you can close it systematically.

High-Capacity Self
  • Clear on priorities and sequence
  • Acts before motivation arrives
  • Recovers quickly from setbacks
  • Decisions feel grounded
  • Tolerates discomfort without escaping
  • Evidence-based self-trust
  • Output matches capacity
Low-Capacity Self
  • Reactive, scattered, overwhelmed
  • Waits to feel ready or motivated
  • Derailed by minor setbacks
  • Decisions driven by avoidance
  • Escapes discomfort immediately
  • Self-trust based on feelings
  • Output far below capacity

Section 03

Recovery Phase Tracker

After dropping to low-capacity, you don't jump straight back to high-capacity. There are distinct recovery phases. Knowing which phase you're in changes what actions are appropriate. Doing the wrong actions in the wrong phase extends the recovery period.

Phase 0 — Collapse
Full withdrawal. Energy conservation. No decisions. Rest without guilt.
CURRENT
Phase 1 — Stabilise
Restore basics. Sleep, food, movement. Reduce input, reduce decisions.
CURRENT
Phase 2 — Rebuild
Small wins. Tiny actions. Beginning to collect evidence again.
CURRENT
Phase 3 — Momentum
Consistent output. Evidence accumulating. Self-trust returning.
CURRENT
Phase 4 — Full Capacity
Operating at ceiling. High output, high clarity, high trust.

Section 04

Evidence Log

Self-trust isn't built on affirmations. It's built on evidence — a record of things you said you'd do, and actually did. This is the foundation of the circuit. Start collecting it here.

The rule: Evidence must be behavioural and specific. "I showed up" is not evidence. "I sent the email I'd been avoiding for 3 weeks" is evidence.
