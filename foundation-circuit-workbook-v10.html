Capability vs. Capacity
The Core DistinctionDiagram 01

Everything in the existing framework operates in the thinking and doing dimension. These are cognitive and behavioural frameworks and they are powerful. But they all assume the platform underneath them is stable enough to run them.

This new layer operates in the feeling and being dimension. It is not about what you think or what you do. It is about the state you are in when you try to think and do. And state is not chosen. It is inherited, wired, and managed or mismanaged.

"Capability is what you can do. Capacity is what your system can hold."
Foundation CircuitCore DistinctionAlex Cooper © TSSH

Your capability has not gone anywhere. It is fully intact. What changes across the day is your capacity. When capacity is high, capability loads and runs. When capacity is low, capability cannot load regardless of how strong it is.

4
Version 1 and Version 2
Diagram 02The Arc

We tend to think of our state as relatively stable across a day. But capacity moves. It depletes through decisions, emotional friction, environmental demands, and accumulated stress. By the time the day progresses the platform you are working from is significantly different from the one you started with.

This is what your two versions actually are. Not two different people. The same person operating from two very different platform conditions across the same day.

Version 1  ·  High Capacity State
Full Access
This is you operating above the regulatory threshold. The platform is stable and the full architecture is available. This is when the framework runs cleanly and evidence of achievement is accessible.
  • Clear and accessible
  • Capable and in control
  • Self directed
  • Finds solutions
  • Evidence loads
  • Framework runs
Threshold
Version 2  ·  Low Capacity State
No Access
This is you operating below or near the regulatory threshold. The same capability exists but the platform cannot support it. The framework is inaccessible and threat architecture dominates.
  • Unclear and overloaded
  • Reactive
  • No direction
  • Finds relief
  • Evidence blocked
  • Framework cannot load

For a regulated system, capacity moves like a sine wave. Rhythmic, predictable, sustainable oscillation that stays above the threshold. For a dysregulated system it moves like a sawtooth. Sharp accumulation, sudden collapse, repeat.

Sawtooth : Unregulated
Sharp accumulation then sudden collapse then repeat. Crosses the threshold regularly. No smooth recovery. Version 2 lives here.
Sine : Regulated
Rhythmic, predictable, sustainable. Stays above threshold. Version 1 at its most consistent lives here.

The gap between Version 1 and Version 2 is not character. It is not motivation. It is not weakness. It is regulation.

The Forecasting Error
The Critical Misread

Here is where most of the damage happens. Version 1 wakes up, feels capable, builds a full capacity plan and assigns it across the entire day. A critical assumption is made: that the capacity available right now will hold. It will not. It never does. But the plan is built as though it will.

The Forecasting Error : How It Unfolds
Version 1 Plans

Full capacity plan built from a full capacity state. High load tasks assigned across the whole day. Forecasts that current capacity will remain constant.

↓
Version 2 Arrives

Capacity depleted. Plan exceeds what is available. Cannot execute. Does not register as a capacity problem. Registers as failure. Moral judgement follows. Shame accelerates depletion. Relief behaviours activate.

Version 2 does not see a capacity problem. It sees a personal failing. That misread triggers a shame spiral which depletes capacity further, which makes the plan even less executable, which makes relief behaviours more compelling.

"Version 1 was always forecasting competency. It needs to start forecasting capacity."
Forecasting ErrorAlex Cooper © TSSH

The Two Phase Planning Model
Diagram 03The Threshold

The correction is not motivation. It is not discipline. It is a different planning model. One that accounts for the arc of the day, not just the starting point. One where Version 1 does not hold jurisdiction over the entire day.

Every person has a regulatory threshold. The point below which the system can no longer access its full architecture. The practical model is built around two phases separated by a single critical checkpoint.

Phase 01  ·  Version 1 Jurisdiction
High Capacity Window
  • Deep thinking and creation
  • Difficult conversations
  • High stakes decisions
  • Framework runs cleanly here
  • Full access to evidence base
Between Phases
Capacity Check
"Where am I actually right now?"
Phase 02  ·  Version 2 Jurisdiction
Reduced Capacity Window
  • Routine and completion tasks
  • Physical activity
  • Passive recovery
  • Low cognitive load work
  • Protect the system

The Capacity Check is not a review of what got done. It is a genuine read of where the system actually is before Phase 02 begins. One honest question. Where am I actually right now? Not where Version 1 expected to be. Where you actually are.

"Version 2 is no longer failing to meet a plan. It is operating within a plan designed for its actual state."
Foundation CircuitThe CorrectionAlex Cooper © TSSH

This single shift removes the misread. Which removes the moral judgement. Which stops shame from accelerating the depletion. Which makes relief behaviours less compelling. The plan is not wrong. Its jurisdiction is.
