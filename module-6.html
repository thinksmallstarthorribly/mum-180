Module 06 of 06 — Mum 180

What
Now

Accept your current reality. Change how you think and speak about yourself. You must then act accordingly.

Time: 40–50 min
Sections: 4
Section 1
Accept Your Current Reality

You must accept the current circumstances of your life, honestly, in order to make meaningful change and progress.

Am I saying it's going to be easy? No. Likely it's going to be quite difficult because it's going to create some emotional event.

Emotional events are required to create lasting behavioural change. If you are serious about changing your behaviours, then you must start with yourself, your current circumstances, and some honesty.

You're not perfect. There we go. We said it out loud. You are not perfect, and that is not unique to you. You're a human being, and on some days we feel far more human than we would like. All of us, at some point, need help. There's no reason to persist with something when it's clearly not working.

Reflection
Write an honest assessment of where you are right now. No judgment. No softening. Just the truth.
Section 2
Change How You Talk to Yourself

You must be able to handle the tension of having that conversation with yourself and get comfortable with recognising yourself as a person that requires care, love, and attention — in order to then give it to others.

You're going to have to change the way that you think about yourself. You're going to have to change the way that you speak about yourself. You are the person that you communicate with the most, and the things that you think about the most will express themselves in your actions, behaviours, and inevitably the results you see.

If the results you're seeing in your role as a parent in your adult life are not the ones you want to see, then it is up to you to change that.

Reflection
What does your internal commentary say about you as a mum? Write it down — the actual words. Then write what is actually true.
Section 3
There Is a Rulebook

There is bad data out there. We hear things like: there's no rulebook on life, there's no rulebook on being a parent.

Bullshit. It's called a library. And we have them now in our pocket, in a little glowing rectangle that's connected to the internet.

There are fundamentally core principles around parenting that are not unique to any particular person or the detail of their life. Your primary role as a parent is to prepare that child for the expectations of the adult world and give them enough tools to be able to deal with the preliminary challenges when they get there. You need to be able to demonstrate competency in that role to ensure that outcome.

Section 4
Act Accordingly

Easy to understand can be complicated, most often is. Luckily, that's why we're here — to offer some guidance and support along the way.

You must then act accordingly.

Reflection
What is the most important thing this course has shifted for you — even slightly?
Reflection
What is the one commitment you are making to yourself coming out of this programme? Make it specific.
Reflection
Write a message to yourself — to the version of you who shows up in the mirror. What do you want to say to her?
