Section 01

The Marketplace

You operate in a marketplace — whether you're aware of it or not. The marketplace doesn't care about your effort, your intentions, or your history. It responds to one thing: the value you create and deliver. Understanding the rules of the marketplace is not optional. Refusing to engage with them doesn't protect you. It just makes you invisible.

The five marketplace principles: These are the operating laws of any market you participate in. Select the ones where your current understanding or behaviour has the biggest gap.

✓
1
Value Creation
The marketplace rewards those who create genuine value for others — not those who simply work hard or have good intentions.
✓
2
Personal Responsibility
You are the variable. The marketplace doesn't owe you results. Your outcomes are a direct reflection of the value you're creating and delivering.
✓
3
Dynamic Marketplace
The marketplace changes constantly. What worked before may not work now. Adaptability and real-time adjustment are competitive advantages.
✓
4
Trading on Value
Every transaction — job, client, relationship — involves an exchange of value. Your ability to articulate, demonstrate, and deliver that value determines your position.
✓
5
Competitive Advantage
Your competitive advantage is the unique intersection of what you do well, what others need, and what most others can't or won't deliver.
Reflection 1.1
Which marketplace principle do you most resist, avoid, or disagree with? What does that resistance tell you about where your value gap actually is?

Section 02

Hierarchy of Value

There are five stages of perceived value in any marketplace. Most people are stuck at Stage 1 or 2 — not because they lack skill, but because they can't communicate and demonstrate their value in a way the market can read. Click on the stage that most accurately describes your current position.

MY STAGE
1
Recognised
Others know you exist. You have presence, a profile, some awareness. But you haven't yet demonstrated specific, relevant value.
MY STAGE
2
Facilitated
You can get things done for people when asked. Value delivery is reactive — you need to be prompted, directed, managed.
MY STAGE
3
Requested
People specifically seek you out. Your reputation precedes you in a specific area. Value delivery is proactive and trusted.
MY STAGE
4
Preferred
Given a choice, people choose you over alternatives. You have clear differentiation and strong evidence of superior outcomes.
MY STAGE
5
Standard of Excellence
You define the benchmark. Others reference you. Your category leadership is established and self-reinforcing.
Reflection 2.1
What is the specific evidence that places you at your current stage — and what is the specific gap between your current stage and the next one up?
